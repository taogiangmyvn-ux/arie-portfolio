01 · The Challenge

A price-and-speed category, a scattered customer base, no ad budget.

Cross-border delivery is a commodity: customers compare on price, speed and reliability. Amamy's customers — Vietnamese students and families abroad — were spread across countries, each with its own culture and moment. Growth had to come from relevance and trust, not spend.

Commodity

Hard to stand out when everyone competes on price and speed.

Fragmented

Customers in Germany, Japan, Australia, the US — different needs, seasons, language.

No budget

Growth had to be earned through content, referral and community.

How do you build a trusted brand across markets when you can't buy the reach?

04 · Customer Journey

From a nervous first shipment to a default habit.

Tap a stage to expand · scroll sideways →

Awareness01

Who ships my package?

In their head
“Mom’s care package… who do people here actually use?”
Touchpoints
Market-fit social · community events · student groups
Friction
Never heard of the brand.
My move
Show up where the diaspora already gathers — embassy events, student groups. In a trust category, presence beats ads.
Signal I watch
Mentions & asks inside community groups.
In a trust business, get introduced — don’t interrupt.
Consideration02

Can I trust them with my parcel?

In their head
“If this box disappears, three months of gifts are gone.”
Touchpoints
60K fanpage · reviews · transparent pricing
Friction
Fear of lost or delayed shipments.
My move
Make trust visible before the first order — a living fanpage, real customer stories, prices published upfront. No “inbox for price” games.
Signal I watch
Message-to-quote rate, review sentiment.
Transparency converts the anxious better than any promo.
First send03

Is this worth trying once?

In their head
“Fine. One small box. If it arrives, we’ll talk.”
Touchpoints
Voucher at events · clear pricing · easy booking
Friction
Effort and perceived risk of a first attempt.
My move
Shrink the first yes — event vouchers, simple booking, small trial parcels. Trying costs almost nothing but proves everything.
Signal I watch
Voucher redemption, first-order completion rate.
Don’t sell the lifetime. Sell the first safe arrival.
Repeat04

Do I come back next time?

In their head
“Arrived in six days. Saved. That’s my shipping company now.”
Touchpoints
Reliable delivery · seasonal reminders · loyalty
Friction
No reason to prefer us next time.
My move
Own the calendar — Tết, back-to-school, gifting season — with reminders that land exactly when the need does.
Signal I watch
Repeat rate per season, reorder interval.
The habit is seasonal — win the moments, win the year.
Refer05

Should I tell a friend?

In their head
“My roommate ships next month. I could just send her my link.”
Touchpoints
“Send in Pairs” · double-sided reward · community
Friction
No easy, rewarding way to share.
My move
Make the reward symmetric — 5€ for both sides — so sharing feels like gifting a friend, not selling one out.
Signal I watch
Referral share of new senders, invite acceptance.
Word of mouth scales when both sides win.

Content built awareness. Trust, reliability and a referral loop turned one shipment into a habit — and a habit into new customers.
