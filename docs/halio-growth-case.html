01 · The Challenge

The original growth model was running out of room.

Early U.S. traction leaned on Vietnamese-American creators and diaspora communities. Effective to start — but the same pool couldn't power the next stage, and repeated patterns made fresh reach harder.

Audience ceiling

The diaspora creator pool was finite and increasingly saturated.

Creative ceiling

Content needed native U.S. language, settings, routines and use cases.

Commercial ceiling

First purchases weren't enough — growth needed basket size, adoption and retention.

How could Halio expand beyond its niche without losing credibility or leaning on deeper discounts?

Lever 01

Expand the addressable audience

Scout mainstream U.S. beauty, wellness and lifestyle creators. Select by consumer need and use case, not demographic similarity.

04 · Customer Journey

From first attention to long-term value.

Tap a stage to expand · scroll sideways →

Awareness01

Why should I care?

In her head
“Another skincare gadget. Scroll.”
Touchpoints
Native creators · PR · events · paid discovery
Friction
Diaspora-flavored content reads “not for me” to the mainstream buyer.
My move
Stop translating Vietnamese creative. Brief U.S.-native creators to open on the problem — dull skin, harsh actives — before the device ever appears.
Signal I watch
3-second hold rate by segment — not raw reach.
Relevance is won in the first 3 seconds, in her language.
Interest02

Is this relevant to me?

In her head
“Looks high-tech. Probably complicated. Probably not for my skin.”
Touchpoints
U.S. hooks · problem-solution · routine integrations
Friction
Tech-first framing reads technical or unnecessary.
My move
Sell a 4-minute routine, not a technology. Every asset answers one question: where does this fit in her night?
Signal I watch
Saves & profile visits — curiosity, not applause.
Curiosity follows familiarity — show the routine, skip the spec sheet.
Consideration03

Can I trust it?

In her head
“$149 for something I might use twice? Prove it.”
Touchpoints
Reviews · tech explainers · comparisons · email
Friction
Price, credibility, confusion vs. lookalike devices.
My move
Stack proof in order — expert explainers, side-by-sides, 30-day results — then let email answer objections one at a time.
Signal I watch
Branded search volume & email CTR on proof content.
Trust isn’t claimed. It’s stacked, one objection at a time.
Purchase04

Worth buying now?

In her head
“I want it. Maybe next payday.”
Touchpoints
Product pages · bundles · retargeting · cart recovery
Friction
Price, no urgency, unclear routine value.
My move
Offer architecture over discounting — bundles that complete the routine, cart recovery that restates value instead of shouting a coupon.
Signal I watch
AOV & bundle attach rate, not just CVR.
Raise the basket by completing the routine, not cutting the price.
Adoption05

How do I get value?

In her head
“Week two. Am I even doing this right?”
Touchpoints
Post-purchase email · usage & frequency · routine content
Friction
Inconsistent use, wrong expectations — silent churn before results show.
My move
A 30-day onboarding flow that sets expectations week by week and celebrates the first visible result. An unused device never reorders serum.
Signal I watch
Flow engagement, support tickets, early refund rate.
The sale isn’t done at checkout — value happens in week three.
Repeat06

My next reason to return?

In her head
“The wand works. What goes with it?”
Touchpoints
Gel-mask launch · cross-sell · replenishment · win-back
Friction
No routine, no relevant next product.
My move
Launch consumables the device makes better — the gel mask turned one-time device buyers into replenishment customers.
Signal I watch
60/90-day repeat rate, LTV by cohort.
The device wins the customer. Consumables build the lifetime.
Advocacy07

Worth recommending?

In her head
“My friend asked what I use. Where do I send her?”
Touchpoints
Reviews · referrals · UGC · affiliate · community
Friction
No simple advocacy path.
My move
Time review prompts to first results, give real fans affiliate codes, feed UGC back into ads as proof.
Signal I watch
Referral share of new customers, UGC per 100 orders.
Happy customers are a channel — if you build them a door.

The first purchase created revenue. Adoption, routine expansion and lifecycle created the opportunity for long-term value.
