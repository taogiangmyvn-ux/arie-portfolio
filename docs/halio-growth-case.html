01 · Context

The original growth model was running out of room

Halio's early U.S. traction relied heavily on Vietnamese-American creators, diaspora communities, familiar cultural networks, and creator-led social commerce.

This was an effective starting point. But the same audience and creator pool could not support the next stage of growth indefinitely. As more suitable creators were contacted or activated, the available pool became smaller — and repeated content patterns made it harder to generate fresh reach.

Relying heavily on first-time acquisition would not create sustainable growth unless customers also adopted the products, built routines, returned for complementary products, and stayed engaged with the brand.

The growth plateau
01

Audience ceiling

The Vietnamese-diaspora creator pool was finite and increasingly saturated.

02

Creative ceiling

The existing content system needed more native U.S. language, settings, routines, and consumer use cases.

03

Commercial ceiling

Acquisition could generate first purchases, but sustainable revenue also required stronger basket size, product adoption, and long-term engagement.

How could Halio expand beyond its initial niche without losing credibility or becoming dependent on deeper discounts?

02 · Diagnosis

The problem was not one channel. It was the whole growth system.

The next stage of growth required more than adding creators. The audience, creative system, commercial offer, media ecosystem, and customer lifecycle all needed to evolve together.

Layer 1Audience

Move beyond a primarily diaspora-led audience toward broader U.S. beauty, wellness, lifestyle, and at-home skincare consumers.

Layer 2Creative

Create U.S.-native content that felt natural in American feeds and translated product technology into relatable routines and use cases.

Layer 3Commercial Offer

Move from isolated product selling toward clearer, higher-value routines and bundles that could improve basket size.

Layer 4Lifecycle

Support first-time buyers after purchase through education, usage guidance, complementary-product introduction, and retention communication.

03 · Strategy

Four growth levers, one connected system

01

Expand the addressable audience

"Find more creators similar to previous winners"→"Find new audiences who share the same underlying consumer need"
Challenge

The original creator pool was becoming saturated.

Actions
  • Broaden scouting to mainstream U.S. beauty, wellness, fitness, lifestyle and pro-skincare audiences
  • Recruit native-English creators who speak naturally to U.S. consumers
  • Explore archetypes beyond the diaspora network
  • Select creators by consumer need and use case, not demographic similarity
  • Build a scalable creator acquisition & activation workflow
Journey role · Awareness & Consideration
02

Build U.S.-native creative assets

Translated product communication→Culturally native consumer communication
Challenge

Translated or generic product-review content would not earn relevance with broader American consumers.

Message territories
  • At-home consistency between professional treatments
  • Maintaining results without repeated clinic visits
  • Beauty tech inside a weekly self-care routine
  • Recovery & wellness use cases
  • Premium device value vs. repeated pro services
Actions
  • Adapt hooks, scripts and briefs to natural U.S. language
  • Use familiar locations, routines, pain points and behaviors
  • Develop specific use cases, not generic reviews
  • Build assets that work organically and through paid amplification
  • Explain the technology through everyday benefits
Journey role · Awareness, Interest & Consideration
03

Increase basket size through offer architecture

Selling individual devices→Selling a complete beauty-technology routine
Challenge

A single-product approach limited order value and did not always make the complete routine clear.

Actions
  • Pair complementary products into understandable routines
  • Position bundles as more complete solutions, not simple discounts
  • Communicate why products work better together
  • Use seasonal, gifting and campaign contexts
  • Align creator, product-page, paid and email messaging on one offer
Lift & Light Duo — $499One bundle, three commercial jobs: make the routine easy to understand · raise average order value · create more product touchpoints for future engagement.
Journey role · Consideration & Purchase
04

Turn first purchase into long-term value

First-order revenue→Customer lifetime value
Challenge

A high volume of first-time buyers would not automatically create long-term growth.

Actions
  • Build welcome and product-education journeys
  • Develop browse, cart and checkout recovery
  • Use post-purchase email for usage, frequency and expectations
  • Reinforce routine formation
  • Introduce complementary products and future launches
  • Use retention, replenishment, cross-sell and win-back
Device purchase → product education → consistent usage → routine formation → gel-mask introduction → complementary-product adoption → repeat engagement

04 · Media System

Earned media built credibility. Paid media scaled demand. Owned media built the relationship.

Earned

Organic creator content, PR moments, events, a New York Fashion Week-related activation, Miss Universe talent, and cultural relevance.

Purpose — generate credibility, attention and social proof.

Paid

Creator-content amplification, Meta Collab Ads, whitelisting, retargeting, and conversion-focused scaling of winning creative.

Purpose — expand reach and scale proven messages.

Owned

Brand social channels, website, product pages, email marketing, educational content, community, and launch & retention communication.

Purpose — control the narrative and hold the long-term relationship.

No single media layer could solve the business challenge alone. Growth came from letting each layer play a different role across the customer journey.

07 · Transformation

09 · Learnings

What I learned about scaling a brand

01

Scale does not always come from finding more of the same audience.

When an acquisition pool reaches saturation, the next growth opportunity may require redefining the addressable audience and rebuilding creative relevance.

02

Brand, media, offer and lifecycle cannot be optimized in isolation.

A stronger campaign cannot compensate for a weak offer, unclear product value, or an incomplete post-purchase journey.

03

First purchase is a revenue event. Product adoption is a retention event.

Long-term growth depends on whether the customer understands the product, experiences its value, builds a routine, and has a relevant reason to return.

My role was not simply to activate more creators. It was to help evolve the system around how Halio reached, converted, educated, and retained customers in the U.S. market.
