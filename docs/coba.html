01 · The Challenge

A beautiful brand story is not a growth model.

CoBa's Daughter had a rich heritage — Vietnamese ingredients, ritual, craft. The task was to make that story win on a crowded global shelf, where shoppers scroll fast and discount noise is everywhere.

Relevance

A heritage story had to land with a broad, discerning US audience — fast.

Clarity

Premium products needed to read as a complete ritual, not scattered SKUs.

Repeat

One-time gifting had to become a returning routine, without leaning on price.

How do you scale a heritage brand on a global marketplace without discounting away the premium?

The Brand World

Positioning you can feel before you read.

The insight: women who ride — who want low-maintenance luxury that keeps up with a real life. Every frame was art-directed around it — heritage, equestrian craft, sensorial ritual — so the premium reads in a single scroll.

04 · Customer Journey

From a scroll to a ritual worth repeating.

Tap a stage to expand · scroll sideways →

Discovery01

Is this my kind of brand?

In her head
“Pretty packaging. Everyone has pretty packaging.”
Touchpoints
Affiliate creators · brand world · Amazon & TikTok Shop discovery
Friction
Reads as just another body-care brand.
My move
Lead with heritage and editorial imagery no lookalike can copy — the brand world differentiates before price enters the picture.
Signal I watch
Profile visits & follows per 1K reach.
In a copycat category, the story is the moat.
Consideration02

Is it worth the premium?

In her head
“$38 for a scrub? Convince me.”
Touchpoints
Creator reviews · ingredient story · ritual demos
Friction
Price vs. unclear routine value.
My move
Reframe product into ritual. Demos show the full sequence, so the price compares to a spa evening — not a drugstore scrub.
Signal I watch
Watch-through on demo content, PDP time on page.
The ritual justifies the premium. The ingredient list never will.
Purchase03

Which one do I start with?

In her head
“Six products. I’ll think about it.” — and she leaves.
Touchpoints
Product pages · bundles · gifting sets · seasonal offers
Friction
Too many SKUs, no clear entry point.
My move
Name the first step — starter ritual sets with a clear “begin here,” gifting bundles for Q4. One decision instead of six.
Signal I watch
Bundle share of first orders, first-order AOV.
Choice kills conversion. A named first step saves it.
Ritual04

How does this fit my week?

In her head
“Loved it Sunday. Forgot it by Thursday.”
Touchpoints
Post-purchase education · usage rhythm · refill prompts
Friction
Bought once, habit never formed.
My move
Anchor usage to a weekly moment — Sunday-ritual content and flows that teach rhythm, not just product facts.
Signal I watch
Opens on ritual content, time between orders.
A product used weekly reorders itself. One used once never does.
Retention05

Time to restock?

In her head
“Almost empty. Reorder — or try something new?”
Touchpoints
“Twice the Ritual” refills · replenishment · cross-sell
Friction
No timely reason to reorder.
My move
Refill pouches at a kinder price, replenishment emails timed to real usage cycles — reorder becomes the path of least resistance.
Signal I watch
Refill attach rate, reorder interval vs. product lifespan.
Retention is a timing problem before it’s a loyalty problem.
Advocacy06

Worth sharing?

In her head
“This feels like something my sister would love.”
Touchpoints
Community events · co-brand moments · UGC · referrals
Friction
No shared moment to talk about.
My move
Create moments worth photographing — co-brand events, giftable rituals — then feed that UGC back into acquisition.
Signal I watch
Tagged UGC per month, referral-attributed orders.
Word of mouth needs a moment, not a discount code.

The shelf ranking got attention. Ritual, refills and community made the brand worth returning to.
