01 · The Problem

The problem was not a lack of information.

Vietnamese families have endless health content — but it is fragmented, technical and contradictory. Parents know nutrition matters; they don't know what to do at the moment they choose, cook or serve food.

Notice a concern → search Google / social → conflicting advice → try a quick fix → hard to sustain → back to old habits
Primary persona

Working mother, 26–35 — wants practical meal decisions without becoming a nutrition expert, on a busy schedule.

Information alone does not change behavior. Guidance must arrive at the moment of the food decision.

02 · The Product

Make healthy eating understandable — and actionable.

If nutritional information becomes visual, contextual and personalized, parents are far more likely to understand it and use it. Four value pillars:

01

Understand

Translate complex nutrition into clear visual information.

02

Decide

Recommend one practical next step for the household.

03

Plan

Turn single decisions into a manageable meal routine.

04

Improve

Help families see progress and build healthier habits.

The MVP bet

Prove behavioral value, not feature volume. The first version had to show a family could understand the result and make a better food decision — nothing more.

Household profile → AI/AR scan → simplified result → one action → save → return
