From first booking to repeat habit.

A leaky-funnel diagnosis: how might a delivery platform lift first-booking activation — then turn it into retention by making the second booking meaningfully easier than the first?

01  Friction mapping 02  Cohort drop-off diagnosis 03  Hypothesis-driven UX experiments

Independent portfolio analysis based on publicly observable product experiences and stated assumptions. Not an internal AhaMove project; recommendations have not been confirmed as implemented. No confidential data is used.

01 · Persona & Job To Be Done

The merchant is not booking a driver. They are protecting a customer promise.

Functional job

"Book a suitable delivery fast, so I can fulfil the order without stopping my business."

Emotional job

"Feel confident the delivery won't damage my relationship with my customer."

Social job

"Look reliable and professional — even with a team of one."

Persona: a small online seller in Ho Chi Minh City, ~5–20 orders a day, comparing providers on speed, price and reliability, with little time to troubleshoot.

The opportunity: become part of the merchant's daily operating habit — not just one delivery option among many.

02 · Friction Mapping

Confidence dips are where merchants churn.

Merchant confidence across the first booking — hypothesized

Product friction

Too many service choices · repeated address input · no first-time guidance — stalls activation.

Trust friction

Final price? Fast match? Safe handling? Who helps if it fails? — delays commitment.

Growth friction

No reason to return · second booking isn't easier · features stay hidden — blocks feature-led retention.
