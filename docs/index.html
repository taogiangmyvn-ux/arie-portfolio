How I Think

One operator, the whole funnel.

Most marketers own a slice. Running two brands end to end means I work every stage — and know how each one feeds the next.

The calls — decisions I'd make again
Kill what doesn't compound

Sunset the loyalty platform

A community-commerce platform looked good on paper but wasn't earning its keep. I built the cost-per-real-conversion case, recommended sunsetting it, and pointed the budget at channels that were actually compounding.

Sunk cost is not a strategy.
Retention by design

1 refill = 2 jars

CoBa's refill system isn't packaging — it's a retention loop. Buying one refill locks in the second ritual, so repeat purchase is built into the product before a single retargeting ad is needed.

LTV starts at the product, not the ad.
Loops beat pushes

Double-sided referral

Amamy's "Send in Pairs" gives 5€ to the referrer and the referee. Single-sided referrals stall; symmetric rewards made sharing feel like gifting — one engine behind a 60K-like fanpage and a ×1.5 revenue year.

Make the user the channel.
The rules — operating principles
01Attention is earned in the first second. The hook is the strategy — everything after it is production.
02Organic proves it, paid scales it. If a concept can't spread without budget, budget won't save it.
03The product is the first ad. A refill system retains better than any retargeting campaign.
04Creators are channels with economics. I negotiate payouts and commissions like a CFO, not a fan.
05Every campaign ends in a number. If nothing moved, it was art — not marketing.
