01 · The Challenge

A campaign is only real when the shelf agrees.

Retail is where brand promises meet reality. A promo exists in a deck, a banner, a shelf strip, a cashier's script — and it only works when all of them say the same thing, on the same day, in every store.

Many moving parts

POSM, mechanics, digital assets and store teams — each on its own timeline.

Partner standards

Global brands like L'Oréal audit execution. "Almost right" fails the check.

Zero slack

Opening dates and campaign windows don't move. Retail runs on the calendar.

How does one message survive the trip from HQ deck to store shelf?

04 · Shopper Journey

From walking past to walking back.

Tap a stage to expand · scroll sideways →

Pass by01

Why walk in today?

In her head
“Another drugstore. I have three of these near home.”
Touchpoints
Storefront · opening events · official-store social
Friction
Nothing signals “today it’s worth it.”
My move
Run the opening as an event, not a date — first-50 gifts and a 100% lucky draw give a reason to cross the door today, not someday.
Signal I watch
Opening-day footfall vs. a normal Saturday.
A store opening is a campaign with a deadline.
Step in02

Where do I even look?

In her head
“I came for sunscreen. What’s this wall of promos?”
Touchpoints
POSM · floor layout · staff talk-track
Friction
Promo noise; the ad and the shelf don’t match.
My move
One message per zone — the key visual, the shelf strip and the staff line say the same thing, checked before day one.
Signal I watch
Mechanics uptake — do shoppers actually join the promo?
In retail, consistency is the creative.
Browse03

Which brand do I trust?

In her head
“La Roche-Posay — or the cheaper one next to it?”
Touchpoints
Partner campaigns · brand blocks · testers
Friction
Shelf parity — everything claims the same thing.
My move
Let the partner’s equity work — co-branded assets to Guardian’s audience, executed to the brand’s global standard.
Signal I watch
Partner sell-through during campaign windows.
The chain brings traffic. The brand brings trust. The campaign marries them.
Basket04

Is the deal real?

In her head
“The banner said gift. The cashier better know about it.”
Touchpoints
Promo mechanics · bundles · checkout comms
Friction
A promo that fails at the till kills trust twice.
My move
Execution follow-up — verify the mechanics work in-store before the campaign goes live online. Asset readiness, then store readiness.
Signal I watch
Redemptions without complaints.
A promise kept at the till is the cheapest loyalty program.
Return05

Back here next time?

In her head
“They had my brand, the gift was real. This is my store now.”
Touchpoints
Official-store social · next campaign · membership
Friction
Retail memory is short.
My move
Always-on official-store content keeps the next reason visible between visits — the calendar never goes quiet.
Signal I watch
Engagement on campaign posts, repeat-visit proxies.
Retail loyalty is remembered convenience.

One campaign gets her in the door. Execution at the shelf decides if she comes back.
